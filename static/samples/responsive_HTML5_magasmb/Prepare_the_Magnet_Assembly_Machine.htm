Prepare the Magnet Assembly Machine

The heating element of the magnet assembly machine is 6 in. wide.

CAUTION: The bottom and top of the heating element space are hot to the touch. The sides of the heating element space are not hot.

The heating element is arranged in three sets of walls. The wall position gives the assembled magnet its shape. The magnet has 18 layers. The bottom wall forms the first six layers; the middle wall forms the next nine layers; the top wall forms the final three layers.

CAUTION: Activating the wall switches will move both sides of the magnet assembly machine and the heating element space.

  1. Clean the heating element surface with a scraper and isopropyl alcohol.

Note: Do not clean the heating element surface with any tools or materials except a scraper and isopropyl alcohol.

  1. Cut a clean sheet of 6-mil plastic to 72 × 50 in.

Note: The plastic sheet is a shield that protects the machine from the epoxy and a mold release that prevents the magnet from sticking to the machine. Use a new sheet of plastic every time you prepare the magnet assembly machine.

  1. Place the plastic across the machine and press it down into the heating element space.

  2. Tuck the plastic into the space so that the plastic covers all metal edges of the heating element space.

Figure: Plastic Sheet Placed in Heating Element Space

  1. Place the spacer in the bottom of the heating element space at the uphole end of the machine.

Figure: Initial Spacer Placement

Note: The spacer will not fit in the machine in the wrong direction. Spacers only fit in the uphole end.

  1. Confirm that the plastic sheet is as straight and flat as possible. Make sure there are no creases or folds in the plastic sheet.

The machine is prepared for assembling the magnet.
