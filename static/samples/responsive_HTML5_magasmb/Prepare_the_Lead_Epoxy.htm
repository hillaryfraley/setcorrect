Prepare the Lead Epoxy

The lead epoxy used to assemble the magnet is a mixture of lead, epoxy resin, and hardener. This procedure describes how to prepare the lead and epoxy resin by mixing them. You will add the hardener later (see Add Hardener to the Lead Epoxy).

Note: The can of lead epoxy already contains both lead and resin. You have to remix the lead and resin in the can because the lead settles during shipping and storage.

  1. Use a syringe to remove 100 cc of resin from the can.

  2. Discard the syringe of resin in the hazardous materials collection bin.

  3. Use a can opener to remove the rim from the top of the can.

Note: Removing the rim helps the mixing blade fit in the can and makes it easier to add hardener after the lead and resin are mixed.

  1. Place the can onto the mixer and secure the can in place.

  2. Lower the mixing blade into the can so that the tip of the blade is approximately 1 in. below the surface of the resin.

  3. Turn on the mixing blade.

  4. Adjust the blade speed to 270 rpm.

  5. Slowly lower the blade into the epoxy until the blade is immersed.

  6. Allow the lead and epoxy resin to mix at 270 rpm for 2 hours.

While you wait for the lead epoxy to mix, continue with the next procedure, Prepare the Magnet Assembly Machine.
